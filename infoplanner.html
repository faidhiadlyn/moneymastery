Planning


Saving money involves setting aside a portion of income rather than spending it immediately. It's a financial practice aimed at building a financial cushion, meeting future goals, or preparing for unexpected expenses, promoting financial stability and long-term financial well-being.


How to start saving ?

Buy second-hand goods.

If you take the time to search through the used goods at your nearby bundle or other charity shops, you might be pleasantly surprised by the finds. You could find inexpensive, high-quality clothing, interesting small ornaments, vintage accessories, or antique books.

Make your own meals.

One of the costliest activities is eating out, particularly if you are a student in a location with pricey dining options. Making your own food is not only more affordable but also healthier and better for your body.

Take advantage student discounts.

While you're shopping, present your student ID at the register to have a tiny portion of your entire order deducted. Students can get discounts in restaurants, bookstores, electronics stores, clothing stores, and eateries.

Apply for scholarships.

For example, JPA, MARA, Maybank, and Bank Rakyat are a few renowned scholarships that are available to Malaysian students. A fantastic method to lower tuition is to apply for any kind of scholarship, grant, or bursary.

Live together with others.

Divide your rent to save money each month. Seek for a location near your school or inquire about any shared on-campus accommodation options. This is a really cost-effective approach for you and your pals to save money. You can cut costs on food when you live with pals.

Find a part-time job.

Having a part-time job has several advantages, like being one of the finest methods to save money quickly and not having to give up your social life. Apps that make money such as Grab, Shopee, Foodpanda, and Lalamove also assist you in earning extra money so you may conserve.

Use public transport.

You can save money and cut down on expenses by taking public transportation such as MRT or carpooling with friends. Bus or train cards are widely available in many cities and nations, and they can greatly reduce your travel expenses.

Learn understand wants and needs.

Being able to distinguish between needs and wants makes it simple to learn how to save money as a student. Long-term financial savings as a student may result from realizing that what you want may not be what you need.


Starting little today can help you maintain your motivation as you save money.
If you can see the end result in sight, you'll be more motivated to put in the footwork of saving.


Why we should saving ?

1. Financial Security
Saving money provides a financial safety net, offering security in case of unexpected expenses, emergencies, or changes in income. It helps prevent reliance on debt and provides a buffer against financial uncertainties.

2. Goal Achievement
Saving enables individuals to work towards and achieve financial goals such as buying a home, funding education, or starting a business. Regular savings contribute to the gradual accumulation of funds needed to realize these aspirations.

3. Peace of Mind
Having savings instills peace of mind by reducing stress related to financial uncertainties. Knowing that there's a financial cushion in place allows individuals to navigate life's challenges with greater confidence and less anxiety.
